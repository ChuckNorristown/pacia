PACIA meeting minutes

Friday  10.11.2002

Philadelphia Police  //  8th District

The meeting was called to order at 10:30am by Recording Secretary John Kelly who then lead the membership in the pledge of allegiance to the flag. Members of the board present were Sam Dunlap, John Kelly, Salvatore Gerace, Mike Ebner and Michele Morris.


There were approximately fifteen members present at the meeting. The weather was very poor and may have been a factor in the poor turn out for the meeting.


Secretary Kelly then called for reports from committees. The treasurers report was given by Sam Dunlap who reported from the financial statement of the PPFCU that the balance of savings was $45313.98 and Checking of $3446.45. There were not reports given from other committees except for the Nominating Committee, Hugh O'Donnell. He indicated that he had not received a good response from the nominations ballets that were sent to the membership and has only received about 11 returns. He states that he will prepare the ballots which will be presented to those present at the November meeting for election of officers and board members.


There was no discussion of old business from the membership. Under New Business, Secretary Kelly advised the membership of a message he had received via the internet from the Maryland State Insurance Fraud Division. They are conducting an inquiry into a group of individuals who are operating up and down the east coast. This group is identified as an ethnic group who are making claims of injuries while they are eating in restaurants. They are operating in couples who identify themselves as a married couple. The female usually makes the claim of glass or a foreign object ingestion. The names most often used by the couple are Mary Dixon, Nancy Stevens, Lisa Moore, Mary Evano, Mary Reed, Lisa Benton, Mary Evans. The male has used the names of Ronald Ross, Ronald Evano, Robert Dixon, Ron Rose, Ronald Dixon, Ronald Evans, Ron Evan, Robert Evans, Robert Evaro, Paul Lee Nicholas. A list of ssn's is available for the individuals. If you have any information contact Jim Gaylon, Insurance Fraud Division at 410.230.4209.


Secretary Sam Dunlap advised the members reports system which is currently under development by the City of Philadelphia, Records Department. The system is identified as PINN and will be developed over the next two years. It will enable subscribers to obtain Philadelphia Police Reports via the internet. The system will encompass additional reports from the current system which only enables you to obtain accident reports. The extent and scope of the new system is currently under development between the City of Philadelphia and Ciber, Inc., Custom Solutions Group, of Wayne, PA. The points of contact for further information are Philadelphia Records Department at 215.686.2261 and Theodore E. von Mechow II, of Ciber, Inc. at 610.993.8100.


The Guest Speaker for the meeting was Mike Ebner, who is now an investigator with Independence Blue Cross. Mike explained the overview of the nature of the Blue Cross systems and the volume of operations which they encompass. The operate in the five county area of Philadelphia with 4.2 million subscribers and handle over 32 million claims a year. They have 7000 employees. The fraud they investigate usually involves several hundred thousand dollars on each investigation.


The meeting was adjourned with the next scheduled meeting scheduled for Friday 11.8.2002 @ 10am, Philadelphia Police 8th District, Red Lion & Academy Roads, Philadelphia, PA 19114.


The minutes are respectuflly submitted by John Kelly  //  Recording Secretary  //  PACIA.
